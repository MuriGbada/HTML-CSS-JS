Chief Samuel Ladoke Akintola

The lawyer, orator, journalist, the 13th Aare Onakankafo of Yorubaland and the second Premier of Western Nigeria. (b:10th July, 1910 - d:14th January, 1966)

.

Lists of accomplishments

  • He became a legal adviser to the Action Group, the dominant Western Region party, and by 1954 was deputy leader under Chief Obafemi Awolowo.
  • He became minister of labour in 1952 and later held the portfolios of health, communications, and aviation.
  • As the Minister of Health he facilitated the completion of the University Teaching Hospital Complex, Ibadan (UCH), between 1954-1956 and was able to release the sum of 10 Million Pounds to aid its quick relocation, from the then Adeoyo Hospital, established in 1926, which had housed the Hospital.
  • He was thereafter made Minister of Communication and Aviation, and he was instrumental to the founding of Nigerian Airways to compete with the West African Airline Company (WAAC) and British Overseas Airways Company (BOAC), that had hitherto dominated the aviation sector in Nigeria.
  • He was installed as the 13th Aare Onakakanfo of Yorubaland, and the 1st in the 20th century.
  • He fought to established the University of Ife (now OAU) at Ile-Ife, a city considered the source of Yoruba and became its 1st chancellor
  • He was awarded the Grand Commander ofthe Order of the Niger (GCON), the second highest national award in Nigeria.
  • He was a nationalist who fought for the independence.
  • He formed the Nigerian National Democratic Party
  • He was a prime factor in the contested election 1965 which was undoubtedly an immediate cause of the January 1966 coup in which he was slain.
